12. What are the characteristics of the people who have paid back?¶

Data Analysis Summary¶

After complete analysis of the current dataset, we can start to draw conclusions regarding the type of people who are most likely to pay off their loans. This is crucial to the operation of this bank, as with such information, we can make better decisions and make more money through lending money. The following information is profiles of people likely to pay their loans and those who are likely to default.

Likely to Pay Back

  1. Checking Account
    • Those with a higher checking account status are more likely to pay back
    • Interesting observation: There were many people who had paid back with an 'unknown' checking account status
    • Particularly, those with a status of '2' are most likely to pay back

  1. Amount

    • A lower loan amount indicates the person is more likely to pay back
    • The average loan amount of those who pay back is $1,000 lower than the average of those who don't

  2. Duration

    • A shorter loan duration indicates the person is more likely to pay back
    • The average duration of those who pay back is 5 months lower than the average of those who don't
    • Duration seems to have one of the largest impacts on one's ability to pay off
    • I would recommend highly considering duration when evaluating applicants

Likely to Not Pay Back

  1. History

    • A lower history status indicates the person is less likely to pay back
    • A majority of applicants have history of '2' status
    • Those who are most probably to pay back have a '4' status and second to that is a '2' and '3' status

  2. Age

    • A lower loan amount indicates the person is more likely to pay back
    • The average age of those who paid back is higher than the average age of those who don't
    • Most applicants seems to be in their 30's

  3. Employment

    • Most fall within the '2' status range, which mean they worked at their current employer for between 1 and 4 years
